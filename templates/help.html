Help & Support

Welcome to the BreatheEase help center. Here you'll find answers to common questions and resources to make the most of your experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use the Mood Test?

Simply navigate to the Mood Test page and type how you're feeling in the text box. Be honest and detailed—share your emotions, thoughts, and what might be causing them. Our system will analyze your input and provide personalized recommendations including calming music, mindful activities, and inspirational quotes.

How accurate is the mood analysis?

BreatheEase uses sentiment analysis technology to interpret the emotions in your text. While it's quite accurate, it's important to remember that it's a tool to support you, not replace human understanding or professional mental health care. The more detailed and honest you are in your description, the better the recommendations will be.

Is my information private?

Yes. BreatheEase does not store your personal information or the text you submit. Your mood analysis is processed in real-time and not saved to any database. Your privacy and comfort are our top priorities.

What if I'm in crisis?

If you're experiencing a mental health emergency, please contact emergency services immediately or reach out to a crisis hotline. BreatheEase is a supportive tool but is not equipped to handle crisis situations. See our crisis resources below.

Can I use BreatheEase instead of therapy?

No. BreatheEase is designed to complement mental health care, not replace it. If you're struggling with persistent mental health issues, we strongly encourage you to seek support from a qualified mental health professional who can provide personalized treatment and care.

How often should I use BreatheEase?

You can use BreatheEase as often as you need—daily, weekly, or whenever you're seeking support. Many users find it helpful to check in with themselves regularly to track their emotional patterns and practice the recommended mindfulness activities.

Tips for Better Results

  • Be specific: Instead of "I feel bad," try "I feel anxious about my upcoming presentation"
  • Express emotions: Use feeling words like sad, worried, peaceful, excited, numb
  • Provide context: Share what might be influencing your mood
  • Be honest: There's no judgment here—authenticity leads to better support
  • Take your time: Reflect on your feelings before typing
